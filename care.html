Get Started

Neurodiversity-Affirming Care is a way of supporting people with different types of brains, like those with autism, ADHD, or other neurological differences. Instead of trying to "fix" people, this approach values their unique strengths, respects how they see the world, and understands that challenges often come from an unsupportive environmentâ€”not from the person themselves.

Key parts of this approach include:

  • Focusing on what you're good at, not just what's hard.
  • Working together to change the environment so it's easier to navigate and more inclusive.
  • Creating a safe, accepting space where you feel understood.
  • Helping you learn about yourself, know what you need, and advocate for yourself.
  • Offering support that fits your individual needs and respects your way of thinking.
